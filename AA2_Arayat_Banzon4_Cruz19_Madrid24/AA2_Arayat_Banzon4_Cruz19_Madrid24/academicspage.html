Curriculum


With an emphasis on STEM, River Academy takes immense pride in its students' mastery of the arts and sciences. Teaching grades 7 through 12, we also provide engaging and instructive extracurricular activities to help students branch out into different fields.

River Academy follows the country’s core curriculum, offering students the chance to develop their skills in literature, art, mathematics, and etcetera. The Core Curriculum is divided into seven Learning Areas. Languages, Literature, Communication, Mathematics, Philosophy, Natural Sciences, and Social Sciences are among them. The SHS curriculum incorporates current information from various General Education areas.

Local culture, history, and reality are used to inspire examples, activities, songs, poetry, tales, and pictures. This makes the teachings more relevant to the students and easier to comprehend.Through continuity and consistency across all levels and courses, students gain in-depth knowledge, skills, values, and attitudes.

While there are one or two quarterly exams every quarter, there are also opportunities for students to generate written work and use performance activities to show what they know and are capable of. The number of written assignments and performance tasks depend on the subject, although they must be distributed throughout the quarter and utilized to evaluate students' mastery of each course.

Grading System

Students' academic success in the Philippines is gauged by the grading system. It is crucial for students to comprehend how the grading system operates in order to establish reasonable objectives and monitor their development.

The Philippines' elementary and secondary school grading systems employ a 5-point grade system. The minimum passing grade is 2.50, which is comparable to a C. A rating of 90 or higher is considered outstanding. Students' academic success is represented by numerical values assigned by the grading system. The Philippine grading system, in contrast to some others that employ a pass/fail methodology, assesses students' work in light of their academic advancement.

The minimum grade in the K–12 system is 60, however the report card will have a converted rating of 75. When it comes to the final and quarterly grades, the lowest possible score for a student is 60. The top 10 students in each grade with a general average of 90 or higher will be considered for honors from grades 7 through 12. A final grade or final rating of less than 85, as well as a grade of less than 80 in each quarter, are requirements for honors candidates.

Parent-teacher conferences, when the report card is addressed, are a quarterly means of presenting parents and guardians with an overview of their child's development. Though meetings can be scheduled for individuals who would like to ask more questions regarding their child's academic progress, remarks are delivered at the conclusion of each quarter.

About Us

Established in 1987, we are one of the top medical sciences based academies in Quezon City. Our founder Dr. George Tolentino I wanted to create a hub for advancing medical knowledge and fostering collaboration among healthcare professionals. With a commitment to excellence, we continue to empower the next generation of medical practitioners through innovative education and research initiatives. We aspire to raise empathetic, compassionate, capable, and skilled professionals who will lead the medical field to a brighter future.
